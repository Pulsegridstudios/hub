## Overview

This guide explains how ownership, usage rights, and asset permissions are handled within PulseGrid Studios projects.

It applies to all developers, contributors, testers, staff members, and anyone who creates, submits, or accesses project content.

The goal is to protect:

- The project
- The development team
- Individual contributors
- Studio assets
- Scripts, models, UI, maps, and systems
- The long-term future of PulseGrid Studios games

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> This page is intended as an internal policy guideline. It is not a replacement for formal legal advice.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to make sure that all work created for PulseGrid Studios projects is handled clearly and fairly.

This includes:

- Who may use project assets
- How developer contributions are handled
- What developers may use in personal projects
- What happens when someone leaves the team
- What content may or may not be reused
- How ownership disputes are reviewed
- How third-party assets should be handled

> 💡 **Tip**  
> Clear ownership rules help prevent confusion, disputes, and asset misuse later.

---

## Project Ownership

PulseGrid Studios retains ownership and control over official studio projects, branding, systems, and game experiences.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- Game names
- Logos and branding
- Roblox places and experiences
- Core project structure
- Maps and environments created specifically for the project
- UI designs created specifically for the project
- Game mechanics and systems
- Documentation
- Internal tools
- Official Discord and community materials

Developers may be given access to work on these projects, but access does not automatically grant ownership of the full project.

> ⚠️ **Warning**  
> Access to a project does not mean ownership of that project.

---

## Developer Contributions

Developer contributions may include:

- Scripts
- Modules
- UI
- Models
- Textures
- Animations
- Sounds
- Maps
- Buildings
- Documentation
- Game systems
- Design ideas
- Testing feedback

By contributing work to an official PulseGrid Studios project, the contributor gives PulseGrid Studios permission to use, modify, publish, maintain, and continue using that work within PulseGrid Studios projects.

This permission remains in place even if the contributor later leaves the development team, unless a separate agreement says otherwise.

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Contributors should only submit work they have created themselves or have permission to use.

---

## Developer-Created Assets

If a developer creates an original asset themselves, they may continue to use that asset in their own personal projects, unless a separate agreement says otherwise.

This may include:

- Models
- Textures
- UI elements
- Animations
- Sounds
- Scripts
- Systems
- Tools
- Written documentation

However, by submitting that asset to an official PulseGrid Studios project, the developer gives PulseGrid Studios permission to use, modify, publish, maintain, and continue using that asset within PulseGrid Studios projects.

This means:

- The developer may still use their own original work elsewhere
- PulseGrid Studios may still use the submitted work in its projects
- Neither side should falsely claim full exclusive ownership unless agreed in writing
- The asset must not contain stolen, leaked, or unauthorised third-party content
- The developer must not take or reuse assets created by other team members without permission
- The developer must not copy full project files, private builds, or combined studio work for personal use

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Developer-created assets remain usable by the developer in personal projects, but PulseGrid Studios also retains permission to use the version submitted to the project.

---

## Asset Rights

Assets made for PulseGrid Studios projects are handled based on who created them and what permissions were agreed.

If a developer creates an original asset themselves, they may use that asset in their own personal projects unless a separate written agreement says otherwise.

By contributing an asset to an official PulseGrid Studios project, the developer grants PulseGrid Studios permission to use, edit, publish, maintain, and continue using that asset as part of PulseGrid Studios projects.

Project-specific versions, edited versions, integrated systems, private files, internal builds, and combined project work should not be copied, redistributed, resold, leaked, or reused without permission.

Project assets may include:

- Custom models
- Custom scripts
- Custom UI
- Custom textures
- Custom audio
- Custom animations
- Written documentation
- Internal tools
- Configuration files
- Game data structures

> ⚠️ **Warning**  
> Developers may use their own original assets, but they must not take private project files, combined studio work, confidential systems, or assets created by other team members without permission.

---

## Use of Studio Assets

Studio assets may only be used for authorised PulseGrid Studios work.

Developers may use studio assets when:

- Working on an approved project
- Testing an approved feature
- Fixing bugs
- Creating approved updates
- Preparing official content
- Working within assigned responsibilities

Developers must not use studio assets for:

- Personal Roblox games, unless they created the asset themselves
- Other development groups, unless permission is given
- Public releases outside PulseGrid Studios, unless permission is given
- Selling or trading
- Reuploading under another account
- Claiming the work as their own if they did not create it
- Sharing with unauthorised users

> 💡 **Tip**  
> If an asset came from a private project file and was not created by you, treat it as a protected studio asset.

---

## Third-Party Assets

Third-party assets must only be used if they are allowed under their licence or terms of use.

This includes assets from:

- Roblox Creator Store
- Marketplace packs
- Free model libraries
- External websites
- Sound libraries
- Texture libraries
- Open-source code
- Community creators

Before using third-party content, developers should check:

- Whether public use is allowed
- Whether attribution is required
- Whether editing is allowed
- Whether redistribution is allowed
- Whether the asset is safe and legitimate
- Whether it is suitable for use in a PulseGrid Studios project

> ⚠️ **Warning**  
> Do not use stolen, ripped, leaked, or unauthorised assets in PulseGrid Studios projects.

---

## Open-Source Code

Open-source code may only be used if the licence allows it.

Developers must be careful when using code from:

- GitHub
- Forums
- Tutorials
- Public scripts
- Documentation examples
- Community resources

Some licences may require attribution, source disclosure, or other conditions.

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> If you are unsure whether open-source code can be used, ask senior staff before adding it to the project.

## Voluntary Contributions

PulseGrid Studios development is currently voluntary.

This means contributors are helping the project by choice and should only contribute work they are happy to provide under these guidelines.

By submitting voluntary work to an official PulseGrid Studios project, the contributor allows PulseGrid Studios to use, modify, publish, maintain, and continue using that contribution within PulseGrid Studios projects.

Voluntary contributors should:

- Be clear about what they are contributing
- Only submit work they have permission to use
- Ask questions before submitting major work if unsure
- Understand that submitted work may remain part of the project
- Respect the work of other contributors

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Voluntary contribution does not remove a developer’s ability to use their own original assets in personal projects, unless a separate agreement says otherwise.

---

## Leaving the Team

When a developer leaves the team, they must continue to respect project ownership, confidentiality, and asset rights.

If a developer created an original asset themselves, they may continue to use that asset in personal projects unless a separate agreement says otherwise.

However, former developers must not:

- Reuse private project files
- Reupload full project builds
- Share internal scripts or systems
- Leak development content
- Claim ownership of the full project
- Remove or damage existing project work
- Use assets created by other developers without permission
- Use confidential systems in other games
- Sell or distribute studio-owned content
- Share private Discord, Roblox Studio, or internal document content

Work already submitted to the project may remain in use by PulseGrid Studios unless otherwise agreed.

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Leaving the team does not remove PulseGrid Studios’ permission to use work that was submitted to the project.

---

## Portfolio Use

Developers may ask for permission to show selected work in a portfolio.

Portfolio use may be allowed if:

- The content has already been publicly released
- It does not reveal private systems
- It does not include confidential information
- It does not expose scripts or security systems
- It is presented honestly
- Permission has been granted by senior staff

Developers may usually show assets they personally created, but they should still avoid showing unreleased project content, private builds, internal systems, or anything that could reveal confidential information.

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Permission should be requested before using PulseGrid Studios project work in a public portfolio.

---

## Prohibited Use

The following actions are not allowed:

- Copying project assets created by other developers
- Selling or trading studio assets
- Uploading studio content under another account
- Sharing private files with non-staff
- Reusing confidential scripts elsewhere
- Claiming someone else’s work as your own
- Removing creator credit where required
- Using stolen or leaked assets
- Publishing private development builds
- Sharing internal documentation publicly
- Reusing full project systems, maps, or builds without permission

> ⚠️ **Warning**  
> Serious misuse of assets may result in removal from the team and further action where appropriate.

---

## Credit and Recognition

PulseGrid Studios aims to give fair credit where appropriate.

Credit may be given through:

- Staff lists
- In-game credits
- Discord credits
- Update logs
- Contributor mentions
- Portfolio permission

However, credit does not automatically grant ownership, control, or permission to reuse project assets created by others.

> 💡 **Tip**  
> Credit recognises contribution, but ownership and usage rights are handled separately.

---

## Disputes and Reviews

If there is a disagreement about ownership, credit, or asset usage, it should be reviewed by senior staff.

Reviews may consider:

- Who created the asset
- When it was created
- What it was created for
- Whether permission was given
- Whether the asset uses third-party content
- Whether the asset was submitted to the project
- Any written agreements or messages
- The impact on the project

> ℹ️ **Note**  
> Keep records of important agreements, especially for major contributions or shared assets.

---

## Final Notes

Ownership and asset rights are important for protecting the project and everyone who contributes to it.

Developers should only contribute work they have the right to use and should only use project assets for approved PulseGrid Studios work.

Developers may continue to use their own original assets in personal projects, but they must not take private project files, confidential systems, combined studio work, or assets created by other team members without permission.

> ⚠️ **Warning**  
> If you are unsure whether you can use, share, copy, or modify something, ask senior staff first.

---

## Agreement

By contributing to or accessing PulseGrid Studios projects, you agree to respect the ownership, asset rights, and usage rules described in this policy.

By submitting work to a PulseGrid Studios project, you allow PulseGrid Studios to use, modify, publish, maintain, and continue using that work within PulseGrid Studios projects.

Developers may still use their own original assets in personal projects unless a separate agreement says otherwise.

Failure to follow these rules may result in removal from the development team, loss of project access, Discord moderation action, or further action where appropriate.
